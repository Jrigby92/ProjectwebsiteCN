Joseph Rigby | Tech consultant



1.5

Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software
– how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?








My basic method would be to conduct as much research as possible, in an efficient way and from accurate sources.

My first port of call would be to use google in order to gather a basic understanding of the options available, I could use their advanced search methods such as: Putting search terms in inverted comments for exact results, using OR between search queries to broaden my results, or adding “Related:” before search queries to find similar sites to the one which was searched for. To strengthen my knowledge further, I would reach out to other people in the industry by real world networking, or connecting via LinkedIn and other professional social media platforms. If possible, I could attend real world learning events such as ‘Digital Transformation Expo Manchester’ or online learning platforms such as Guava Script.

Having collected enough knowledge on IT systems and software, I would cross reference my findings with anyone I had met (via the methods listed above) who had conducted similar projects to my own. And finally, by using free trials I could

run tests on the products / systems I had shortlisted to ensure they were appropriate for the tasks at hand.

When it came to the point of starting the project and putting the selected IT systems and software into use, if the money was available I could employ a specialist software consultants on a freelance basis to ensure the team / myself were trained in making the best use of it. Alternatively for smaller projects / budgets, I could use YouTube to learn how to maximise the potential of said IT systems or software applications.
