3.3 Develop solutions to improve own productivity using IT in digital projects





























If given the option, in the future I would certainly prefer to carry out projects in teams.


I believe it improves my own drive and productivity, along with giving a clearer understanding from the beginning of the project of how much time investment is required to complete. This is because each avenue of tasks can be worked on In parallel. And if it’s discovered the writing is taking longer than the web designing for example, the roles of each team member could be shifted to create a balanced timeline for each project goal. Ideally, resulting a unified completion of the goals in the most efficient manor. It’s also clear that for the majority of people working in a team motivates each other to fulfil their own part of the project, since we are all social animals at heart.


In such a scenario I would certainly employ zoom, slack and trello to keep a team unified and properly in sync with each other. There is much research on how important ‘soft skills’ are to complete projects, and the aforementioned software certainly creates an environment where soft skills can flourish. I would be keen however, to see if zoom would employ all of the features of slack or visa versa. In this case I would be eager to switch platforms to the most comprehensive.

If given the same project again, to be completed alone. I would certainly ease my use of the trello board. Along with designing my website in a more efficient way, as explained in unit 3.2.
